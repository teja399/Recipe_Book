About

Chow mein are Chinese stir-fried noodles with vegetables and sometimes meat or tofu. Over the centuries, variations of Chaomian were developed in many regions in China; there are several methods of frying the noodles and range of toppings can be used.

Steps

    Noodles

  1. firstly, in a large vessel take water.
  2. add 1 tsp salt, 3 tsp oil. get the water to a rolling boil.
  3. now add 3 pack noodles and dip completely.
  4. boil for 3 minutes, or refer to package instructions to know the cooking time.
  5. separate the noodles and drain off.
  6. rinse with cold water to stop cooking and turning noodles mushy.

    7. Chow Mein Sauce

  7. firstly, in a small bowl take 1 tsp corn flour, 1 tsp chilli powder, ¼ tsp cumin powder and ¼ tsp coriander powder.
  8. also add 1 tsp sugar, ¾ tsp salt, 2 tbsp soy sauce and 2 tbsp vinegar.
  9. further, add 2 tsp oil and ¼ tsp pepper powder. traditionally, sesame oil and oyster sauce is also added.
  10. mix well making sure there are no lumps, chowmein sauce ready.

    11. Chow Mein

  11. firstly, in a large wok, heat 2 tbsp oil and stir fry 3 clove garlic, 1 inch ginger and few spring onion.
  12. add ½ onion, ½ green capsicum, ½ red capsicum, ½ carrot and ½ cup cabbage.
  13. stir fry until the vegetables shrink yet remain crunchy.
  14. make sone space in the centre and pour the prepared sauce.
  15. stir fry on high flame for a minute until everything is well combined.
  16. also, add ½ cup cabbage and stir fry.
  17. further, add boiled noodles and give a good toss.
  18. make sure the spices are well combined with noodles.
  19. finally, add more spring onions, and enjoy veg chowmein.
